Using Satellite Imagery to Investigate Rapid Constructions Around Nephrite Mine in eastern Nangarhar Province, Afghanistan

The left satellite image, Figure 1, shows a nephrite mining site in Goshta District of Nangarhar Province from 2019, while the right image, Figure 2, captures the same site in 2023. The rapid constructions visible in the 2023 image illustrate substantial development in the area since the Taliban returned to power in August 2021.

Introduction

The Taliban Islamist group swept to power in Afghanistan in August 2021 following the collapse of the West-backed government under President Mohammad Ashraf Ghani and the chaotic withdrawal of the US forces.

Then, the international community and the US imposed sanctions on the Taliban de facto government as it failed to form an inclusive government and observe human rights, especially women’s rights.

Consequently, Afghanistan lost billions of dollars in humanitarian aid and the Taliban resorted to extracting mines to revive the fragile economy. Satellite imagery tools, including Google Earth Pro, Google Earth Engine Timelapse and Google Earth Engine Code Editor are of significance for journalists to write investigative stories about legal and illegal mining in countries like Afghanistan where domestic media outlets are forced to do self-censorship.
